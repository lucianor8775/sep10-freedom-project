    Fun Facts

    The Sun

    The Sun, our closest star, is incredibly massive and hot, containing 99.86% of the mass in our solar system. It's a yellow dwarf star, about halfway through its life cycle.

    Jupiter

    Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined. It rotates in under 10 hours and has faint rings.

    Mars

    Mars is known as the Red Planet due to its iron oxide-rich soil. It has the tallest volcano, Olympus Mons, and the deepest canyon, Valles Marineris.

    Hardware

    Telescope

    Telescopes allow us to observe distant planets, stars, and galaxies by collecting and magnifying light. They are essential tools for both amateur and professional astronomers.

    Spectroscope

    A spectroscope splits light into its component colors, helping astronomers determine the composition, temperature, and movement of celestial objects.

    Space Probe

    Space probes are unmanned spacecraft sent to explore the solar system and beyond, gathering data from places humans cannot yet reach.

    Radio Telescope

    Radio telescopes detect radio waves from space, allowing astronomers to study phenomena like pulsars, quasars, and cosmic microwave background radiation.

    Astronaut Suit

    Astronaut suits protect space travelers from extreme temperatures, radiation, and lack of oxygen, enabling them to work safely outside spacecraft.

    Software

    Stellarium

    Stellarium is a planetarium software that shows a realistic sky in 3D, just like what you see with the naked eye, binoculars, or a telescope.

    Celestia

    Celestia lets you explore our universe in three dimensions, traveling throughout the solar system and to over 100,000 stars.

    NASA Eyes

    NASA Eyes is an interactive visualization tool that lets you explore Earth, the solar system, and spacecraft missions in real time.

    WorldWide Telescope

    WorldWide Telescope is a free, open-source tool that turns your computer into a virtual telescope, bringing together imagery from the worldâ€™s best ground and space telescopes.

    Space Engine

    Space Engine is a 3D astronomy program and planetarium that lets you explore the universe, from planets and stars to entire galaxies.
